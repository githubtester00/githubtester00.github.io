The James Webb Space Telescope

User Feb 09, 2022

The Big Bang took place 13.7 million years ago and created everything and everyone we know. Which in turn, started the cycle of curiosity along with it. Humans developed over time, surviving, socialising, colonising, learning, and passing knowledge.

Space is full of perplexing mysteries, which we humans have always been eager to unravel, until now. That curiosity might be at its final stage, thanks to NASA, ESA (European Space Agency), and CSA (Canadian Space Agency) latest collaboration project.

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) that took a whopping 10 billion dollars over an extremely challenging and lengthy course of 25 years to develop, is the most powerful telescope to ever exist. The project was initiated in 1996. The telescope was launched on 25 December 2021 from ESA’s launch port in the French Guiana and is currently in the second Lagrange point (L2) in the Solar Orbit. The telescope is currently 1.5 million kilometres far from its home, Earth.

The primary objective of the James Webb Space Telescope is; to study every phase in the History of our Universe such as the mystery of the Big Bang, and help discover stars which are enshrouded by clouds of dust, explore the history of the assembly of galaxies in the early Universe, which is the birth of stars and planets, and look for other neighbouring life forms. Webb will also support and extend the discoveries made by the Hubble Space Telescope, a space telescope operating before it.


Instruments


Mirror: Webb consists of a huge and shimmering mirror which is made of 18 separate hexagonal shaped segments which are foldable at their sides. These hexagonal mirrors are made of Beryllium and are also coated with 24-carat Gold to improve the mirror’s reflection of Infrared rays.

Optical Telescope Element (OTE): The OTE is the eye of the James Webb Space Telescope. It gathers the light coming from Space through the mirrors and forms a clear image.

Spacecraft Bus: This is the transport mechanism of the JWST. It helps the satellite to travel in space. It also manages the other functions of the satellite such as Power, by taking energy from the solar panels installed on the JWST.

Infrared: The telescope has advanced Infrared Camera with focus on different lengths and ranges to detect even the most minute and distant heat signatures.

Sunshield: The James Webb Space Telescope has a 5 layered sunshield as its centre unit. These shields protect Webb from great amounts of heat which are emitted by the Sun, Earth and Moon. The sunshields are foldable as well.

Integrated Science Instrument Module (ISIM): This is the main payload of the telescope. It manages the Infrared Camera, Infrared Spectrograph, Fine Guidance Sensor, and the Mid Infrared Instrument.

This magnificent piece of technology is expected to provide service for the next 10 years.

This glorious technology has huge expectations and we cannot wait to see what it holds for us in the future.
